Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services, processes, and elevator solutions.

We offer three tiers of maintenance plans: Basic, Premium, and Comprehensive. The Basic plan includes quarterly inspections and essential maintenance. Premium adds monthly inspections, priority response, and parts replacement. Comprehensive covers 24/7 monitoring, all repairs, and complete parts coverage. We can customize any plan to meet your specific needs and budget.

Installation timelines vary based on elevator type, building complexity, and project scope. A standard commercial elevator typically takes 12-16 weeks from contract signing to final inspection. Residential elevators generally require 8-10 weeks. Custom or high-rise installations may take longer. During your consultation, we'll provide a detailed timeline specific to your project requirements.

All our elevators comply with ASME A17.1/CSA B44 Safety Code for Elevators and Escalators. They're also certified to meet ADA requirements, UL standards for electrical components, and local building codes. Our products undergo rigorous testing for safety features including emergency braking systems, door sensors, and backup power. We provide full documentation of all certifications with every installation.

Yes, we partner with several financial institutions to offer flexible financing options. These include equipment leasing, installment plans, and project financing with competitive rates. For commercial clients, we also provide capital lease structures that may offer tax advantages. Our financial team can help determine which option best suits your situation and assist with the application process.

Our average emergency response time is 30 minutes in metropolitan areas and 60-90 minutes in suburban or rural locations. For entrapments or critical safety issues, we prioritize immediate dispatch. Maintenance contract customers receive priority response. Our 24/7 emergency line connects you directly to technicians, not a call center, ensuring faster resolution of urgent situations.

Yes, we specialize in elevator modernization projects. Our process begins with a comprehensive assessment of your current system to identify components that can be upgraded for improved reliability, energy efficiency, and passenger experience. Modernization can range from control system updates to complete cab renovations. We develop phased implementation plans to minimize disruption to building operations during the modernization process.

We offer several energy-efficient technologies including regenerative drives that capture energy during braking, LED lighting systems, sleep mode controls that reduce power consumption during idle periods, and variable frequency drives that optimize motor operation. These features can reduce elevator energy consumption by up to 40%. Many of our energy-efficient solutions qualify for utility rebates and contribute to LEED certification points.

Elevators should undergo professional inspection at least quarterly, with monthly inspections recommended for high-traffic installations. Annual comprehensive inspections are required by most jurisdictions. Building staff should perform basic visual checks weekly. Our maintenance plans include regular inspections scheduled according to your elevator's usage patterns, age, and local code requirements to ensure optimal safety and performance.

Our standard warranty covers parts and labor for 2 years on new installations. Major components like motors and gearboxes carry extended warranties of 5-10 years. We also offer extended warranty options that can provide coverage for up to 10 years on the entire system. All warranties include quarterly maintenance visits to ensure continued optimal performance and compliance with warranty terms.

Yes, our technicians are trained to service and repair all major elevator brands including Otis, KONE, Schindler, ThyssenKrupp, and many others. We maintain an extensive parts inventory and have established relationships with suppliers for specialized components. Our team regularly updates their training to stay current with the latest elevator technologies across all manufacturers, ensuring we can provide expert service regardless of your elevator's make or model.
